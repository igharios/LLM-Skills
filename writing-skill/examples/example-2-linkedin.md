# LinkedIn Post: Coaching Framework Repurposing

Most engineering coaching fails because it's prescriptive, not principled.

Coaches say: "Do daily standups. Use story points. Run retros Friday."

Better approach: **Find your bottleneck first.**

We watched a team fix their CI/CD (40 → 8 mins). Suddenly daily standups became useful because people had real deploys to discuss. The coach didn't prescribe standups—the constraint created the need.

Good coaching asks: *What's actually slowing you down?*

Bad coaching says: *Do this process.*

The difference? One team understands why. The other just follows rules that break when reality changes.

**Your coaching should trace back to a single goal.** Speed? Reliability? Hiring? If a recommendation doesn't help that goal, it's overhead.

What's your team's constraint right now?
