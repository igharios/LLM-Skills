# Most engineering coaching fails because it's prescriptive, not principled.

Coaches say: "Do daily standups. Use story points."

Better approach: **Find your bottleneck first.**

We watched a team fix CI/CD (40 → 8 mins). Suddenly daily standups became useful—people had real deploys to discuss.

Good coaching asks: *What's slowing you down?*

Bad coaching says: *Do this process.*

Your coaching should trace back to a single goal: Speed? Reliability? Hiring? If a recommendation doesn't help, it's overhead.
