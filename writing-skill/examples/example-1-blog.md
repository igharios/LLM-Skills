# Why Most Coaching Frameworks Fail at Scale

Engineering teams hire coaches to ship faster. But most coaching fails. The framework is too abstract. The recommendations don't apply to your architecture.

**The core problem isn't the advice — it's the framing.**

Most coaching is prescriptive. "Do daily standups." "Adopt story points." "Run retros every Friday." These are tactics, not principles. And tactics break the moment your team scales.

**The better approach starts with constraints, not recommendations.**

What constraints does your team face? Are deploys slow? Is your burndown erratic? The constraint reveals the lever.

Once you find the constraint, you have a principle: **Fix the bottleneck, not the process.**

We watched a team drop their CI/CD time from 40 to 8 minutes. Suddenly, daily standups became useful — people had real updates because they were deploying every day.

**Here's what separates good coaching from cargo cult adoption:**

Good coaching asks questions. Bad coaching prescribes processes.

The teams that improve fastest start here: **What is your team actually optimizing for?** Once you know, every recommendation should trace back to that goal.
