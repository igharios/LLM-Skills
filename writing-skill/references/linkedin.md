# LinkedIn Post Framework

**Audience:** Engineering leaders, delivery practitioners, product people
**Length:** 3–4 paragraphs, ~150–250 words
**Voice:** Sharp, opinionated, direct — one idea per post
**Mode:** Standalone OR asset-promotion (see repurposing-matrix.md for asset-promotion)

---

## Structure

### Paragraph 1 — BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front)
- One sentence. States the topic and the position plainly.
- Not a question. Not a tease. Not a hook that withholds the point.
- The reader should be able to decide in one sentence whether this post is for them.
- Examples:
  - ✅ "Most delivery teams don't have an execution problem — they have a visibility problem."
  - ✅ "Coaching time reclaimed isn't a productivity metric. It's a design outcome."
  - ❌ "Have you ever wondered why your sprints keep slipping?"
  - ❌ "Here's something nobody talks about in engineering leadership..."

### Paragraph 2 — The Tension
- What makes this non-obvious?
- What does the conventional view get wrong, miss, or assume incorrectly?
- This is where the reader's current mental model gets challenged.
- One paragraph. Don't over-explain — name the tension and move on.

### Paragraph 3 — The Argument
- Your actual position, with a concrete example or pattern to ground it.
- Anonymized real scenarios, named failure modes, or field observations.
- The argument should be complete enough to stand without clicking any link.
- One paragraph.

### Paragraph 4 — The Implication
- What should someone do differently, think differently, or stop assuming?
- Scoped to engineering leaders or delivery practitioners specifically.
- No call to action. No "follow me for more." No "what do you think?"
- One paragraph. Close with confidence, not a question.

---

## CTA Rules (Asset-Promotion Mode Only)
- If promoting a Tier 1 asset, the link goes in the **comments**, not the post body.
- LinkedIn suppresses reach on posts with external links in the body.
- Final paragraph earns the link naturally before pointing to comments.
- Example close: "Wrote this out in full with the data behind it — link in comments."
- Never: "Read more here →" or "Check out our latest whitepaper!"

---

## Must Include
- BLUF as the first sentence — always, no exceptions
- One specific, grounded example (pattern, failure mode, or real scenario — anonymized if needed)
- A genuine challenge to received wisdom — not contrarian for its own sake,
  but uncomfortable enough to be worth posting

## Must Avoid
- Questions as openers
- Listicles
- Engagement bait endings ("Drop a comment below", "Agree or disagree?")
- Hedging language that softens the opinion into nothing
- Emojis as structure (bullet-replacing emojis, arrow emojis)
- More than one external link — if there's a link, it's in the comments

---

## Voice Calibration

LinkedIn posts from this account should sound like:
- A practitioner sharing what they've learned, not a thought leader performing expertise
- Direct without being aggressive
- Confident without being dismissive of complexity
- The kind of post you'd screenshot and send to a colleague

Avoid sounding like:
- A consultant selling a service
- A content creator chasing engagement
- An AI writing "valuable content"
